<p><p>The Vehicle Auxiliary Power Unit (APU) market features two primary types: air-cooled and water-cooled systems. Air-cooled APUs use ambient air to dissipate heat, providing a lightweight and simpler design, commonly favored in transport applications. In contrast, water-cooled APUs employ a coolant system to manage temperatures more efficiently, offering better performance in demanding environments. Both types enhance vehicle efficiency by supplying power for air conditioning, heating, and electronic systems without relying on the main engine.</p></p>
