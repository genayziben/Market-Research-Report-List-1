<p><p>The fermented flavours market is primarily categorized into two main types based on the type of bacteria involved: acetic acid bacteria and lactic acid bacteria. Acetic acid bacteria are primarily used in the production of vinegar and sour flavors, enhancing preservation and tanginess in food products. Lactic acid bacteria, on the other hand, are crucial for fermentation processes in dairy, vegetables, and baked goods, contributing to sourness and probiotic benefits. Both types play essential roles in flavor development and food preservation.</p></p>
