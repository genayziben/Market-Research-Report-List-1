In the market, N-channel IGFETs utilize electrons as charge carriers and typically offer higher mobility and greater efficiency, while P-channel IGFETs use holes, providing complementary functionality. Both types are crucial in various applications, including amplifiers, switches, and digital circuits, catering to different circuit requirements and efficiency needs.</p></p>
