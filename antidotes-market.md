<p><p>Antidotes can be categorized into two primary market types: injection and oral. Injection antidotes are administered directly into the bloodstream, offering fast-acting solutions for emergencies, such as opioid overdoses or poisoning. These are typically used in hospital settings for immediate effect. On the other hand, oral antidotes are taken by mouth and may be used for less urgent situations or when intravenous administration is impractical. Each method serves distinct therapeutic needs, balancing efficacy and convenience in treating poisonings or overdoses.</p></p>
