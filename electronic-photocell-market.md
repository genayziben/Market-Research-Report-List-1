<p><ul><li>Center Cathode Photocell</li><li>Center Anode Photocell</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The electronic photocell market primarily features two types: center cathode and center anode photocells. Center cathode photocells operate with a negatively charged electrode at the center, providing enhanced sensitivity to light levels and allowing for quick activation and deactivation based on ambient light changes. In contrast, center anode photocells utilize a positively charged center, which can offer different operational characteristics, such as improved durability and response time. Both types are crucial for applications in outdoor lighting and energy efficiency solutions.</p></p>
