<p><p>Field Effect Transistors (FETs) are semiconductor devices that control current flow using an electric field. The FET market includes two primary types: Junction Field Effect Transistors (JFETs) and Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field Effect Transistors (MOSFETs). JFETs utilize a reverse-biased junction to control current, offering high input impedance and low noise. In contrast, MOSFETs have an insulating oxide layer between the gate and channel, enabling higher efficiency and faster switching speeds, making them essential in modern electronics, power applications, and digital circuits.</p></p>
